Primary Pollutants

When fuel is burned in a vehicle engine, there are various emissions which are expelled through the tailpipe. The most common emissions are:
  • Particulate matter (PM)
  • Nitrogen oxides (NOx)
  • Carbon monoxide (CO)
  • Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs)
  • Lead particles (Pb)
  • Sulphur Cxides (SOx)
  • Carbon dioxide (CO2)

Secondary Pollutants

Some air pollutants are not directly emitted but are formed later through reactions with other elements. These secondary pollutants include:
  • Ground-level ozone (O3)
  • Acid rain
  • Very small particulate matter (PM2.5)
Except for carbon dioxide, all of these pollutants have direct impacts on human health. However, rising concentrations of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere are impacting the environment; indirectly, this will create significant effects on human health and the economy.
