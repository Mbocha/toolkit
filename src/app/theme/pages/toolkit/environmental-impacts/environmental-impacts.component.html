In addition to impacts on human health, air pollution has negative impacts on the natural environment, including: ground-level ozone; acidification; and climate change.
  • Ground-level ozone
  • Acidification
  • Climate change

Emissions of nitrogen oxides and hydrocarbons form ground-level ozone which is toxic to animals and plants. Damage to crops from ground-level ozone can lead to reduced agricultural productivity.

Acidification occurs when sulphur and nitrogen oxide emissions form sulphuric and nitric acid which are deposited into the environment. Impacts can include: reduced agricultural productivity; reduced aquatic life in lakes; and “stressed” forest that either grows slowly or dies altogether. See acid rain for more details.

Climate change is characterized by rising average global temperatures. The majority of scientists, climate experts and governments agree that the effects of climate change will be severe on plant and animal life as well as on virtually every economic sector. Action must be taken. Here is more information on climate change.
