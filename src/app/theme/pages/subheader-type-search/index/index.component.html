Welcome to the Clean Fleet Toolkit

In 2006, United Nations Environmental Programme (UN Environment) and TNT developed the Clean Fleet Toolkit, a practical tool that supported vehicle fleet operators to improve the quality of their fleets.

This Toolkit will help you develop a strategy for reducing the environmental impacts of your fleet of cars, trucks, buses and motorcycles, as well as generators. Many of the options presented also lower costs and improve performance. For more information on the various topics related to clean fleet management, go through the Tools.

IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER:

This toolkit is designed to provide organizations insights into their fleet’s current emissions, impacts and costs, and the relative improvements that different options could provide. The calculations are based on estimations and average emission factors. The actual level of impacts, emissions, costs and savings will be influenced by a number of factors including: driver behavior; road conditions; traffic patterns; vehicle age and condition; maintenance etc.

Reduce your SOx emissions & extend your engine life by switching to lower sulphur fuels.

Switch to Cleaner Fuels

Sulphur oxides (SOx) are gaseous emissions formed by the oxidation of fuel sulphur during the combustion process. They cause or aggravate various health issues, damage vehicle engines and emission control equipment, and contribute to the formation of acid rain. The SOx emission depends entirely on the level of sulphur in the fuel.

The above estimates assume motorcycles can meet basic emission regulations when new (post 1995 US models) and are well maintained. However, 2-stroke motorcycles can have much higher emissions than indicated above when the wrong type or amount of lubricating oil is used and maintenance is poor. This is the case in many developing countries.

2-stroke motorcycles emit more volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and particulate matter (PM) than 4-strokes, mainly due to high emissions of unburned fuel. That is also one of the reasons why 4-stroke engines consume 15%-40% less fuel than 2-strokes.

Offsetting your CO2 emissions can be done by planting trees. It is estimated that between 1 and 7 trees can offset 1 tonne of CO2 over the lifetime of the tree(s). The actual number of trees to offset 1 tonne of CO2 varies, depending on factors such as climate, rainfall, tree species and soil type.

To offset your emissions, you need to plant between {{ (((getAllTotals().carbonDioxide * 1) / 1000) | number : '1.1-1') || "-"}} and {{ (((getAllTotals().carbonDioxide * 7) / 1000) | number : '1.1-1') || "-"}} trees every year your fleet is in use.

This requires between {{ ((((getAllTotals().carbonDioxide * 1) / 1000)/1000) | number : '1.1-2') || "-"}} and {{ ((((getAllTotals().carbonDioxide * 7) / 1000)/1000) | number : '1.1-2') || "-"}} hectares

Example: TNT Dutch and UK fleets:

TNT's Dutch and UK fleets produce 258,000 tonnes of CO2 every year. To absorb these CO2 emissions, it would require planting between 258,000 and 1,806,000 trees every year. This covers between 1,173 and 2,345 hectares, equalling a square area with sides of respectively 3.4 and 4.8km.
